Negative Ramifications

Internet Surveillance

Most people can think of the internet as a mail system. They expect what they send in a "sealed envelope" to only be read by the intended recipient. With physical mail, the recipient would probably be able to at least notice if someone else opened the envelope before them. The internet is a digital existence. There is no way to detect if someone else has made their own copy of what you sent. A couple of the most egregious cases of government surveillance happened in the United States and China. In 2013, Edward Snowden revealed that that the NSA had been listening to of the communications of average American citizens. China has their Golden Shield project where they are actively trying to create a database of information on each of their citizens.

Government Censorship

The next step past listening to everyone's communications is controlling what they can hear. The Great Firewall of China serves to prevent their citizens from viewing anything that goes against the government. France and Germany have bans regarding Nazi propaganda.

Need for Moderation

Surveillance aside, the internet usually provides a large degree of anonymity. This anonymity can lead to a lack of accountability. Also, not having to witness the face of a person you hurt can lead to a desensitization. Words on a screen don't convey emotion. Companies with large public websites are compelled to employ moderators to police their own sites and remove offensive content. This is a thankless job with an very high burn-out rate. The average moderator quits in 3-6 months.

Cyber Bullying

As was stated earlier, anonymity provides a lack of accountability. Some people use this to oppress others. The internet is a content neutral technology. It does not care what it is sending. It just delivers the packages where it is told. This leaves the internet a lot like the wild west. There is no law enforcement. The biggest problem with law enforcement is that it is impossible to implement without surveillance.

Swatting

One of the most dramatic forms of cyber bullying, swatting involves finding someone's home address and calling 9-1-1 to report a fake emergency as a "prank." Having the a SWAT team show up and break into your house to arrest you when you have done nothing wrong would feel awful.
