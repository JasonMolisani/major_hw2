One Big Network

In the early 1970s, a number of different networks had been established. While they all used some form of packet switching, the methods were different. There was no unifying language or protocol. There was no way for the different networks to talk to each other.

Vint Cerf and Bob Khan worked to solve this problem. They defined what is known a the handshake in their paper: Protocol for Packet Network Intercommunication. That name was rather wordy. It was shortened to internetworking. That was shortened to internetting and eventually the term internet was born.
